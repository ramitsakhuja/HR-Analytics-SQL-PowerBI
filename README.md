# HR Analytics

## Project Overview

Developed an interactive HR Analytics Dashboard in Power BI to analyze workforce demographics, employee attrition patterns, compensation trends, and retention drivers.

## Objectives

* Analyze employee attrition across departments and job roles.
* Identify workforce segments with higher turnover.
* Evaluate the impact of overtime, satisfaction levels, tenure, and compensation on employee retention.
* Provide actionable insights for workforce planning and retention strategies.

## Tools Used

* Power BI
* Excel

## Dashboard Pages

### 1. Executive Overview

* Workforce KPIs
* Department-wise attrition
* Gender distribution
* Age group analysis

### 2. Attrition Analysis

* Job role attrition
* Education field analysis
* Marital status analysis
* Tenure-based attrition

### 3. Retention Drivers

* Overtime impact
* Job satisfaction
* Environment satisfaction
* Work-life balance
* Salary band analysis

## Key Insights

* Attrition rate stands at 16.1%.
* Research & Development records the highest employee exits.
* Laboratory Technicians have the highest attrition among job roles.
* Employees with 0–2 years tenure show the highest attrition.
* Overtime employees are more likely to leave.
* Low-income employees account for the highest employee exits.
* Low environment satisfaction is strongly linked to attrition.

## Outcome

The dashboard helps identify employee segments at risk of attrition and highlights key factors affecting workforce retention.
